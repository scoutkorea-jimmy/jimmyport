Privacy Policy

Last updated: 24 June 2026

This site helps people find Scout units, offices, heritage sites, camp sites and event venues on a map. We collect as little personal data as possible, and only what the features below genuinely require. We do not sell your data, run advertising, or use tracking cookies.

1. Who we are

This service (the “Site”) is operated by the Scout Tour Assistant administrator (the “Operator”). For any privacy request, contact privacy@jimmypark.net.

2. What we collect, and why

We only process personal data in the specific situations listed here. If you simply browse the map, we do not ask for or store any personal information about you.

3. Cookies and local storage

We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. The Site uses your browser’s local storage only for functional preferences — for example remembering your chosen nickname, your filters, and a “visited today” flag for the visitor count. This information stays in your browser and is not transmitted to us.

4. Address search

When you search for an address in the suggestion form, the text you type is sent to the OpenStreetMap Nominatim service to look up coordinates. Please avoid entering personal information in that field.

5. Service providers

To run the Site we rely on a small number of providers who may process technical data (such as your IP address) as part of delivering content:

  • Cloudflare — hosting, content delivery and security. Stored data is held in Cloudflare’s key-value storage.
  • OpenStreetMap & CARTO — map tiles and address lookup.
  • Google Fonts — web fonts.

These providers receive your IP address purely because your browser connects to them to load resources. We do not share comment or suggestion content with them.

6. How long we keep data

  • Comments: kept until deleted by you (on request) or by the administrator. Only a limited number of the most recent comments are retained.
  • Place suggestions & moderation logs: kept while needed to review submissions and maintain the data, then periodically pruned.
  • Visitor count: an aggregate number only — it holds no personal data.

7. Your rights

You may ask us to access, correct, or delete your personal data, to restrict or object to its processing, or to withdraw consent at any time. In particular, you can ask us to remove a comment you posted (the “right to be forgotten”); the administrator can delete a comment together with its replies. To make a request, contact privacy@jimmypark.net. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Children

The Site is intended for general use and is not directed at collecting personal data from children. Please do not post comments containing personal details about children or others without their consent.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the Site evolves. The “last updated” date above shows when it last changed.

If anything here is unclear, or you would like a copy of this policy in another language, contact privacy@jimmypark.net.
